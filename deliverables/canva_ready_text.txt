AfterClass Circuit Module — Canva-Ready Card Text

Card 1
Term/Concept: Voltage (V)
Definition: Electrical potential difference that drives current through a circuit.
Real-World Use Case: Determines how fast a phone charges when connected to a wall adapter.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Visual of a battery with a plus and minus terminal labeled V; arrow from high to low potential.

Card 2
Term/Concept: Current (I)
Definition: The rate of electric charge flow through a conductor.
Real-World Use Case: Controls the brightness of an LED or the speed of a fan motor.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Visual of charges moving through a wire with an arrow labeled I.

Card 3
Term/Concept: Resistance (R)
Definition: The opposition to current flow in a circuit element.
Real-World Use Case: Used in heaters or lightbulbs to convert electrical energy into heat or light.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Resistor zig-zag symbol with an arrow showing current being reduced.

Card 4
Term/Concept: Ohm’s Law
Definition: Voltage equals current times resistance in a linear element.
Real-World Use Case: Selecting a resistor to limit current through an LED.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: V = IR triangle (V on top, I and R at the base).

Card 5
Term/Concept: Power (P)
Definition: The rate at which electrical energy is converted or delivered.
Real-World Use Case: Determines the wattage rating of a toaster or laptop charger.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: P = VI and P = I^2R inside a lightning-bolt icon.

Card 6
Term/Concept: Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL)
Definition: The algebraic sum of currents entering a node equals the sum leaving it.
Real-World Use Case: Analyzing how current splits across parallel branches in a circuit.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Node with arrows in and out; label “sum in = sum out.”

Card 7
Term/Concept: Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law (KVL)
Definition: The algebraic sum of voltages around any closed loop is zero.
Real-World Use Case: Solving loop equations in multi-resistor circuits.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Closed loop with a battery and two resistors; plus/minus voltage drops around the loop.

Card 8
Term/Concept: Series Circuits
Definition: Components connected end-to-end with one path for current.
Real-World Use Case: Older holiday light strings where one failed bulb breaks the circuit.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: R_eq = R1 + R2 with two resistors drawn in line.

Card 9
Term/Concept: Parallel Circuits
Definition: Components connected across the same two nodes, creating multiple current paths.
Real-World Use Case: Household outlets where one device fails without affecting others.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: 1/R_eq = 1/R1 + 1/R2 with two resistors drawn in parallel.

Card 10
Term/Concept: Equivalent Resistance
Definition: A single resistance that replaces a network without changing overall behavior.
Real-World Use Case: Simplifying a circuit before solving for current or voltage.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Several resistors inside a box replaced by one resistor labeled R_eq.

Card 11
Term/Concept: Capacitor (C)
Definition: Stores electric energy in an electric field between two plates.
Real-World Use Case: Smoothing voltage fluctuations in power supplies.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Capacitor symbol || with Q = CV shown beneath.

Card 12
Term/Concept: Inductor (L)
Definition: Stores energy in a magnetic field when current changes.
Real-World Use Case: Used in filters and transformers in power systems.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Coil symbol with V = L(dI/dt).

Card 13
Term/Concept: Diode
Definition: A component that allows current to flow in one direction only.
Real-World Use Case: Prevents reverse current in solar panel arrays.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Diode triangle-to-bar symbol with arrow showing allowed direction.

Card 14
Term/Concept: AC (Alternating Current)
Definition: Current that reverses direction periodically.
Real-World Use Case: Standard power delivered from the electrical grid to homes.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Sine wave with positive and negative cycles labeled AC.

Card 15
Term/Concept: DC (Direct Current)
Definition: Current that flows in one constant direction.
Real-World Use Case: Batteries and USB power supplies.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Flat horizontal line labeled DC; compare to AC wave.

Card 16
Term/Concept: Impedance (Z)
Definition: Total opposition to AC current combining resistance and reactance.
Real-World Use Case: Designing AC circuits and filters for desired performance.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Z = R + jX with a right-angle vector diagram of R and X.

Card 17
Term/Concept: Reactance (X)
Definition: Opposition to AC current due to capacitors or inductors.
Real-World Use Case: Tuning circuits for specific frequencies.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: X_C = 1/(ωC) and X_L = ωL with capacitor/inductor icons.

Card 18
Term/Concept: DC vs AC Systems
Definition: DC stays constant while AC alternates, enabling efficient long-distance transmission.
Real-World Use Case: Solar panels generate DC while the grid distributes AC.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: DC line from a solar icon; AC sine wave feeding a transformer icon.

Card 19
Term/Concept: Circuit Ground
Definition: Reference point for voltage and a path for fault current.
Real-World Use Case: Safety grounding in household wiring.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Ground symbol (three descending horizontal lines).

Card 20
Term/Concept: Node and Mesh Concepts
Definition: Nodes are connection points; meshes are loops used for systematic analysis.
Real-World Use Case: Setting up nodal or mesh equations in circuit analysis.
Formula/Visual/Analogy: Simple circuit with nodes labeled A/B/C and a dotted loop labeled mesh.
